This policy is intended to be reasonable and to support the goal of broad

of software that calls itself "Bolyra."

## Permitted uses (no permission required)

You may use the word "Bolyra" without permission to:

1. **Refer to the project accurately**, including in:
   - Documentation, blog posts, talks, papers, and tutorials about the Bolyra
     protocol or software.
   - Statements like "this library implements the Bolyra protocol," "compatible
     with Bolyra," or "based on Bolyra."
   - Comparison tables, benchmarks, and reviews.
2. **Identify a contribution** to the Bolyra project (e.g., "I work on Bolyra").
3. **Use the package names** `@bolyra/sdk` and `@bolyra/mcp` as installed via
   npm — those are how you import the official packages.

## Permitted uses (with attribution)

You may distribute unmodified copies of the official Bolyra packages under the
existing names. Include the `LICENSE` and `NOTICE` files as required by the
Apache License.

## Prohibited uses

You may **not**, without prior written permission from the Trademark Owner:

1. **Name a fork "Bolyra"** or use any name confusingly similar to "Bolyra"
   (e.g., "Bolyra Pro," "Bolyra Cloud," "OpenBolyra," "BolyraJS"). If you
   maintain a fork, give it a clearly distinct name and include a statement
   such as "based on the Bolyra protocol."
2. **Imply endorsement** of your product, service, or fork by the Trademark
   Owner or by the Bolyra project.
3. **Register a domain, social media handle, npm scope, or trademark**
   containing "Bolyra" or a confusingly similar mark.
4. **Use Bolyra logos or visual marks** in a way that suggests an official
   association with the project.

## Compliant implementations of the Bolyra protocol

Anyone may implement the Bolyra protocol from scratch under any license. You
may state that your implementation "implements the Bolyra protocol" or is
"Bolyra-compatible" if it conforms to the published protocol specification.
You may not call your implementation "Bolyra" or use the Bolyra logos.
